# GMPL Chairman's Dashboard — Demo Script
## Meeting Preparation Brief for KJ
**Audience:** Chairman, Archean Group / Good Earth Maritime / Nura Shipco
**Duration:** 20–25 minutes | **Tone:** Confident. Unhurried. Let the product speak.

---

## BEFORE YOU WALK IN

**Have open on your laptop:** `https://gmpl-dashboard.vercel.app/` → Group Overview tab  
**Have on the table:** iPhone with dashboard URL bookmarked as home screen shortcut  
**Have beside it:** iPad (if available) open to the same URL  
**Silence notifications** on all three devices  
**Do not start on slides.** Start on the product.

**One thing to hold in your head:** You are not demoing software. You are showing a Chairman what his mornings, his meetings, and his decisions look like when he has the right tool. Every sentence you say should be about him, not about the dashboard.

## HANDLING QUESTIONS

### "How does it connect to our systems?"

> "In Phase 1, your team uploads structured data — Excel or CSV — through a simple interface. The pipeline handles the rest. There's no replacement of existing systems, no complex integration project. Your team keeps working the way they work. You just get to see the output."

> "In Phase 2, we connect directly to Zoho ERP and your operational systems, so the upload step disappears. But the dashboard works from day one without waiting for that."

### "Who maintains it?"

> "The design and architecture is mine. The integrations are delivered by a development partner. Ongoing, your team maintains the data inputs — which they're already producing. The dashboard logic and AI layer are maintained by the development partner under a support arrangement. You're not dependent on a single person."

### "What does it cost?"

> "I'm in the process of evaluating build proposals from three development partners, so I'll have precise numbers for you within two weeks. What I can tell you is that the architecture I've designed specifically avoids the integration complexity that inflates most maritime software projects — Phase 1 is intentionally simple so you have something working quickly, without a six-month wait."

> "The more useful question is probably: what's the cost of not having it? Your four MR tankers are trading in a market where the difference between a well-managed vessel and a poorly managed one is ten to fifteen thousand dollars per day. This is the tool that closes that gap."

### "Can other people use it — my fleet manager, finance team?"

> "Yes. Role-based access means each person sees only their domain. Your Fleet Manager sees fleet data. Your Finance Manager sees P&L and receivables. Only you see the full group view. The Red Flags tab is your accountability tool — everyone knows their flags are visible to the Chairman."

### "What about security? This is sensitive data."

> "The architecture uses Azure cloud with row-level security — the same infrastructure banks use. Data in transit and at rest is encrypted. The Chairman's account has the only full-group view. Every access event is logged."

### "We use Zoho — does it connect?"

> "Yes. Zoho Books and Zoho CRM have open APIs. The Phase 2 integration pulls your invoices, receivables, and customer data directly. You can see it — there's already a Zoho ERP tab in the dashboard showing what that connection delivers."

## READING THE ROOM

**If he's leaning in and asking questions mid-demo:** Slow down. Let him drive. Answer what he asks, then offer to show the related screen. Don't rush to the Scenario Mapper — if he's engaged on Fleet Status, spend more time there.

**If he seems distracted or impatient:** Accelerate to the Scenario Mapper immediately. Skip Legal & Risk. The Scenario Mapper is the one that creates urgency — it's the moment where a Chairman sees his own business differently.

**If he asks about a specific vessel or situation:** Navigate there directly. Don't say "I'd have to check." Use the dashboard. That spontaneous drill-down is more impressive than any scripted moment.

**If he asks about competitors or benchmarking:** "That's Phase 2. We connect to INTERTANKO and OCIMF datasets, and you can see your fleet's performance against industry averages. Right now the focus is getting your own data visible first — you can't benchmark against an industry if you can't see your own numbers clearly."

**If he mentions another solution they've been quoted:** Don't compete. "I'd be happy to look at that proposal with you. What I'd encourage you to compare is not features — it's time to value. This is running today. And it's designed for one user: you."

## SECTOR KNOWLEDGE — USE ONLY IF IT COMES UP NATURALLY

These are not talking points to insert. They are context to have available if a relevant question arises.

**TCE (Time-Charter Equivalent):** The daily earnings measure for a voyage charter, calculated net of voyage costs (port costs, canal dues, bunker consumption). Allows comparison between different voyage lengths and charter types. Pool operators report pool average TCE. If your vessel's TCE is below pool average, you have an operational issue. If pool average is below spot, you have a pool performance conversation.

**MR tanker market right now:** MR (Medium Range) product tankers are in a structurally strong position. Refinery capacity is shifting East — large refineries being built in the Middle East and Asia mean more clean product moving longer distances to consuming markets in Europe and Americas. Longer voyages = more ton-miles = higher effective demand. The BCTI (Baltic Clean Tanker Index) is the benchmark.

**Pool operators:** Maersk Tankers and Hafnia are two of the three largest MR pool operators globally. Pool operators combine vessels from multiple owners, optimise cargo matching across the pool, and distribute earnings on a pool average TCE basis. The owner gets the pool average, not their individual vessel's voyage result. This is lower risk but caps upside.

**PSC (Port State Control):** Inspections conducted by port authorities in any country to verify vessel compliance with international conventions (SOLAS, MARPOL, MLC). A detention — where the vessel is held in port for deficiencies — is commercially devastating and reputationally damaging with charterers. Oil majors vet PSC records before approving a vessel for their cargo.

**SIRE inspections:** The Ship Inspection Report Programme, managed by OCIMF. Required before most major oil company charterers will fix cargo on a vessel. A vessel without a current, passing SIRE inspection cannot trade with Shell, BP, Aramco Trading, Vitol, or most major charterers. Validity is approximately one year.

**Cosmos river barges:** Inland waterway vessels, typically operating on Indian river systems (Ganga, Brahmaputra). Different commercial model — freight rates per ton, shorter voyages, more crew rotations. Lower capital value than ocean-going vessels but important cash flow contributors. Subject to different regulatory framework (inland waterway authority, not IMO).

**Why the Chairman of an Indian maritime group is based in Dubai / has UAE SPVs:** UAE (Ras Al Khaimah FTZ) offers zero corporate tax, easy repatriation of capital, access to international banking, and no withholding tax on dividends — making it a standard jurisdiction for ship-owning SPVs from the Indian subcontinent. MT Nura Kara and MT Nura Bright being incorporated in UAE RAK FTZ is a deliberate, common, and legal tax structure.
